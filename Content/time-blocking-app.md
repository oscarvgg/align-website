## What is a time blocking app?

A **time blocking app** transforms your to-do list into a realistic, visual schedule you can actually follow. Instead of juggling tasks in your head, you assign each task a specific start and end time — turning vague intentions into committed blocks of focused work.

A great time block app gives you:

- A **visual timeline** so you can see your full day at a glance
- **Drag and resize** to adjust when plans change
- **Reminders** so nothing slips through the cracks
- **Calendar sync** so meetings and tasks share the same schedule
- **Recurring blocks** for routines and habits

Unlike a basic to-do list, a time block app shows you *when* work happens — not just *what* needs to get done.

## How to start time blocking in 4 steps

Whether you use Align or another app, here's how to make time blocking stick:

1. **Audit your fixed commitments** — Block time for meetings, commute, meals, and anything non-negotiable first.
2. **Identify your priorities** — Pick the 2-3 most important tasks for tomorrow. Block time for them before anything else.
3. **Add buffers** — Leave 15-30 minutes between blocks for transitions, overflow, or the unexpected.
4. **Review mid-day** — Look at your timeline around lunch. What's done? What needs to move? Adjust without guilt.

## Is a time blocking app right for you?

A time block app works well if you:

- Have a variable or unpredictable schedule and need to protect focus time
- Struggle with switching between tasks and want clearer boundaries
- Learn visually and want to *see* your day, not just read a list
- Want to build routines that stick without daily re-planning

It's less useful if you mainly need team project management or cross-platform sync (Align currently focuses on Apple devices).
